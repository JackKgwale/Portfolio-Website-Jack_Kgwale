MY PROFILE

Mapping Sustainable Futures: Applying GIS and Data Insights to Strengthen Disaster Preparedness, Enhance Environmental Management, and Foster Sustainable Development

I'm Jack Kgwale, a Geography and Environmental Science graduate with a strong interest in how data, maps, and people intersect to shape more sustainable and resilient communities. My work focuses on using GIS and data-driven tools to tackle real-world challenges, from disaster preparedness to environmental monitoring. Through my leadership in youth-led initiatives like WAFARI, where we’ve raised flood awareness across Africa, I’ve seen the power of combining spatial thinking with community engagement. I’ve led teams, designed surveys, and used maps not just as technical tools, but as instruments of advocacy.

I’m skilled in remote sensing, spatial analysis, and environmental planning, but just as importantly, I care deeply about who benefits from this work. Whether I’m building flood preparedness campaigns, collaborating with researchers, or presenting to policymakers, I bring integrity, curiosity, and a drive to make a measurable difference. Adaptable and globally minded, I’m open to new challenges especially those that push me to grow and create impact beyond my immediate environment.
