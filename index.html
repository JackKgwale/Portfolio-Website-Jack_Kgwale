üåç WAFARI (Water From a Rock Initiative)

Youth-Led Disaster Awareness & Flood Resilience Program

Global Water Partnership | Water Youth Network | Youth-Led Network across Africa

WAFARI is a youth-led initiative aimed at improving flood awareness and resilience in vulnerable African communities through data-driven outreach, disaster preparedness workshops, and targeted surveys. It bridges the gap between climate science and community-level action by translating technical concepts into practical, understandable knowledge for everyday citizens.

  • Leadership Role: Led and co-designed community-centered flood awareness workshops and survey campaigns across South Africa and beyond as part of the Disaster Awareness Team.
  • Cross-Border Data Collection: Conducted surveys in multiple African countries to assess local flood risks and preparedness, building a unique pan-African dataset for youth-led climate action.
  • Campus Mobilization: Organized university-based flood awareness drives and info sessions using visual tools, mapping demos, and personal storytelling.
  • Award Recognition: Recipient of the 2023 Youth Changemaker Award from Global Water Partnership, WMO, and Water Youth Network for innovation and impact in grassroots water resilience.
  • Evidence-Based Action: Used survey findings to tailor localized preparedness strategies and design community-focused workshops based on real needs.

2024

Mapathon 2024
YouthMappers, University of Pretoria

On July 26, 2024, I co-organized and participated in a Mapathon hosted by YouthMappers in the Department of Geography, Geoinformatics and Meteorology at the University of Pretoria. The event focused on mapping buildings, roads, and water pathways in Tongaat, KwaZulu-Natal, following the devastating tornado that struck the region on June 3, 2024. Using the HOT Tasking Manager, we contributed to crucial humanitarian mapping efforts aimed at supporting displaced communities and improving disaster response. The event was held in person and online, drawing participants from across Africa. This Mapathon formed part of the Water from the Rock initiative, a youth-led project I co-founded that promotes access to early warning systems and disaster risk information. The project won the 2023 Global Integrated Flood and Drought Management Competition, and is supported by the WMO, GWP, and Water Youth Network.
